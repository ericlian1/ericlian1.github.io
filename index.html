    About Me

    I am an undergrad pursuing a B.S.E. degree in Computer Science at the University of Michigan, with an intended minor in Ross Business and an expected graduation date of May 2022. I'm an upcoming intern at NASA Goddard Space Flight Center.

     

    I'm broadly interested in Artificial Intelligence and mobile development, as well as media production. I love learning new things and having new experiences, so I'm interested in any opportunities that can create a positive impact on the world.

    Experiences

    July 2019 - present

    Co-Founder, APTIC Accessibility Solutions

    I co-founded APTIC with the mission of empowering visually impaired with autonomy using AI object detection on a mobile app. I helped designed and developed the digital prototype with TensorFlow and Android Studio, along with customer management and marketing campaigns with Adobe Premiere, Facebook Ads and HubSpot.

     

    Born at the European Innovation Academy in Lisbon, an entrepreneurship conference where 500+ students attempt to build their own startups and win Top 10 in 15 days, our team worked together efficiently with daily Scrum meetings and Agile framework, eventually winning the Top 10 and Best Digital Prototype Award.

    September 2018 - May 2019

    Deep Learning Research Assistant, UM-CLASP

    As part of the Undergraduate Research Opportunities Program, I worked in a team of 5 under Professor Mark Flanner from U of M College of Engineering's Climate and Space Science department (UM-CLASP) to improve weather forecasting using deep learning.

    ​

    I spearheaded the project by developing the neural network in Keras/TensorFlow, training it on a Linux Supercomputer through batch PBS protocol using past 38 years (500+ GB) of NASA's MERRA-2 netCDF dataset, and communicating the results through poster and presentations at a research symposium. Our model demonstrated superiority in short-term forecast over NOAA's GEFS model,  and served as a great proof-of-concept for future use of A.I. in weather forecasting.

    May 2019 - September 2019

    Data Science Research Intern, UMSI

    As a summer research intern at U of M's School of Information (UMSI), I worked under the supervision of Dr. Daniel Romero, Dr. Ceren Budak and a PhD candidate to investigate the relationship between messages from Gitter, a chat-room system for Github, and the corresponding repositories' performances using Python. 

    ​

    Specifically, I collected 30+ GB of messages in JSON format from across 13k Gitter rooms through its API, cleaned the data and organized them into csv files using pandas, conducted sentiment analysis with vaderSentiment, visualized data with matplotlib and ran regressions with StatsModel. One of my findings, for example, suggested a positive relationship between sentiment and repo stars. This contributed to the overall research goal of investigating the effect of online collaboration on performances.

    January 2019 - present

    Team Lead, Spade

    Started out as a club project from Michigan Hackers, I'm leading the React Native Team (4+ members) which we named Spade, to develop a mobile application that serves as a communication platform for student organizations and their members. Started out from scratch, I designed the entire UI/UX and implemented backend integration while learning everything on the fly, from React Native and JavaScript, to Firebase and MongoDB. 

    ​

    As the team lead, I am also responsible for communicating with U of M's Center for Campus Involvement, creating the Agile development timeline, delegating the tasks to members, and checking-in on everyone with weekly Scrum meetings. 

    ​

    P.S. The logo is the suit clubs as in "student clubs". We named ourselves Spade as a word-play so as to not sounds too obvious.
